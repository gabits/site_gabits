Visual Graphic Book

Plano de Voo

(Flight Plan)

As an itermediate work for a visual graphics discipline, the proposition consisted on the creation of a physical continuingly foldable book focused on the theme of 'walls'.

The work is set in a series of collages of remarkable pictures, some of them disturbingly showing violence and misery, and others that appear as symbolical along with the visual composition of prision bars and the commom element to all of them, a grey and heavy stone wall.

By personal choice, this work was developed towards the ideia of social and racial segregation in Brazil. Paraphrasing the lyrics of a homonym Brazil national rap song by the popular singer Criolo, 'Plano de Voo' - translated as flight plan, wich in other words could mean a plot idea of an escape -, its conception is directly related to the songwriting and focus on poverty, social exclusion, police repression and life in brazilian favelas.
